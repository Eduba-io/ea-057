01 / The Eduba frame

Most regulated organizations automate the wrong things.

The idea is simple. About 60% of what a managed-care operation does every day is database and code work. About 30% is rule-based logic. Around 10% is a real AI problem. Sorting the operations into those three layers, with explicit governance on the 10%, is the work we propose to scope with the Military and Veterans team.

Applied inside a demonstration contract: network adequacy tracking is a database problem. Prior-authorization rules and eligibility gates are rule-based. Clinical decision-support content, care-manager knowledge, and prior-auth narrative assembly are where a governed AI layer earns its keep, provided the governance is real and documented before DHA sees the output.

Sorting those three layers, with Ethics Engine-style audits on anything touching beneficiaries, is the work we would propose to scope.
